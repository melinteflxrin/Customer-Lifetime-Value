Executive summary

Every customer scored for how likely they are to buy each product, and what that is worth. The result is a prioritised book of cross-sell opportunity.

Targeting lift — the core proof

Cumulative % of buyers reached vs % of customers contacted. Higher above the diagonal = better than random dialing.

Where the next-best-offer lands

Which product wins the recommendation, across 45,211 customers.

Model performance

Three calibrated propensity models, one per product, evaluated on a held-out test set the models never saw.

How well each model ranks & targets

AUC = ranking quality (0.5 random, 1.0 perfect). Top-decile lift = the top 10% buy at N× the average rate.

The signal is real

Term-deposit uptake by occupation. Wide spread around the base rate is exactly what the model learns from.

Method notes

  • Data: UCI Bank Marketing — 45,211 real bank customers.
  • Products: term deposit (real campaign buy/no‑buy), housing loan & personal loan (current holdings, look‑alike).
  • Leakage guarded: call duration excluded — it’s only known after contact, so it can’t inform who to call.
  • Validation: stratified, shuffled folds — the data is time‑ordered, so naive splits invert the scores.
